1. Ujjain: The Spiritual Capital

Key Attraction: The Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga Temple, one of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas, is the city's primary draw.

Bhopal: The City of Lakes

state capital, Bhopal, is a blend of scenic beauty and rich history, split between the historic Old City and the planned New City. PW Live PW Live Key Attraction: Bhojtal (Upper Lake), one of India's largest man-made lakes, and Taj-ul-Masajid, one of Asia's largest mosques. UNESCO Gateways: Bhopal is the primary base for visiting the Sanchi Stupa

Indore: The Commercial and Culinary Hub

Key Attraction: Rajwada Palace and Lal Bagh Palace, which reflect the grandeur of the Holkar dynasty. Experiences: Visit Sarafa Bazaar at night for its world-famous street food scene, including Poha-Jalebi and Bhutta ka Khees

Gwalior: The Fort City

Gwalior is a major urban center known for its regal history and contribution to Indian classical music. Key Attraction: The magnificent Gwalior Fort, often called the "pearl among fortresses in India," which dominates the city's skyline.

Jabalpur: The Gateway to Nature

Situated on the banks of the Narmada River, Jabalpur is the cultural capital of the region and a starting point for wildlife tours.
